# Partner Language Guide

## What Hermes does (approved sentences)
- "Hermes clarifies differences and narrows options by explicit constraints."
- "Hermes provides auditable proof of why options remain or are removed."
- "Hermes is a neutral honesty engine that helps users compare within the store."
- "Hermes repairs expectations by explaining meaningful variation."

## What Hermes does not do (approved sentences)
- "Hermes does not recommend, rank, or optimize choices."
- "Hermes does not promote or feature options."
- "Hermes is not a marketplace optimizer or ad unit."

## Disallowed phrases
- "best option"
- "top pick"
- "optimized results"
- "conversion lift"
- "personalized recommendations"
- "boosted listings"
- "sponsored placement"

## UI microcopy guidance (no preference implied)
Use neutral, explanatory language:
- "Showing options that differ on your selected constraints."
- "These options remain because they match your constraints."
- "Removed because it conflicts with your constraints."
- "Compare differences across remaining options."
- "Change constraints to explore different tradeoffs."

Avoid language that implies preference, persuasion, or optimization.
